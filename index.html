Demos from "Hybrid Silent Speech Interface Through Fusion of Electroencephalography and Electromyography "

Abstract: Silent Speech Interface (SSI) can offer a new way for people to communicate. It is common to study single-modal electroencephalography (EEG) or surface electromyography (sEMG) during silent speech and recognize them or reconstruct into audio signals. However, obtaining complementary information from EEG and sEMG is still challenging. This paper presents a hybrid SSI, a novel converter between bimodal physiological electrical signals and audio signals. EEG and sEMG are fused through two sequence-to-sequence models to achieve complementarity between speech intention and muscle activity during silent speech. The feasibility of the proposed fusion method is validated in the silent speech dataset, and an average objective character error rate (CER) of 7.22% among eight speakers is obtained. The experimental results show that our bimodal-based hybrid SSI facilitates the conversion of physiological electrical signals to audio.
